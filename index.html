Michaelius A'Chapelo is a Scandinavian game developer, musician and programmer.

My dive into game development and music traces back to 2006. I've since earned the degrees B.Sc. in Software Technology and M.Sc. in Digital Media Engineering at the Technical University of Denmark.

My private passion project, Purple Hybrid, has quietly been in development since December 2014. I was Lead Programmer at Oblongata Games on the Android title To Ashes, and my M.Sc. thesis, Annotation in Virtual Reality will be published as a scientific paper in 2021 for its novelty and contribution to the field of computer graphics.

To Ashes is a 2019 Android game available on Google Play.

"The mysterious adventure of a girl made of fire, burning through her parents’ sinister mansion. Dash through levels filled with deranged monuments, cryptic challenges and remnants of the girl’s cheerless past. To flee the estate, you must sidestep traps, uncover secret passages and avoid forever turning to ashes."


I worked as the Lead Programmer, supervising 4 other programmers in a team of 16 developers in total. Responsible for all technical aspects including the end product, I benchmarked the performance of visual technologies in Unity to ensure that phones could display the fiery graphics. I led the programmers in creating tools in Unity to enable our game designers unleash their creative visions for level design.


The Sega Mega Drive (1989) was the first commercially successful 16-bit game console, and also the game console that Sonic the Hedgehog made his debut on.

Purple Hybrid: Arcade (2018) is a small prototypical game running on the Sega Mega Drive. To run the game, download the rom file and load it into an emulator such as Fusion or in your browser.

In Gift of Vision (2018), visibility is your limiting factor. With obstacles divided into two planes - the physical plane with the interior and its traps, and the spiritual plane with ghosts and monsters - can you explore the castle? Whilst always affected by both planes simultaneously, you only see one plane at once.

The game can be played in your browser here (mobile not supported).

This Unity game was developed in a three-man group. All visuals and audio are original assets by me.


Liliac Backgammon (2017) is yet another digital implementation of the classic board game.

The unit-tested game logic ensures a correct implementation of all Backgammon rules, with the interface provided by the MonoGame engine. The game is assumed to only work on Windows.

A two-man project; all visuals and audio are original assets by me.



Annotation in Virtual Reality is the project behind my M.Sc. thesis in Digital Media Engineering, supervised by DTU Compute's professors in computer graphics.

This thesis explores virtual reality annotation on 3D-scanned objects to compare geometric properties. A virtual reality annotation prototype was developed and compared to keyboard-and-mouse annotation, with low geometric variance as the ideal property.

The thesis concludes with a discussion on how to improve keyboard-and-mouse annotation, following from experience with developing an annotation program in virtual reality.

The project exemplifies how game engines such as Unity also can service user needs. It will be featured in a scientific paper due to its novelty and contributions to digital morphology.
